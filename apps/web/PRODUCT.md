# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Cortex is for people who write and organize durable knowledge in Markdown: builders, researchers, designers, students, and technically comfortable writers who want their notes to remain portable. They arrive at the landing page comparing Cortex with polished knowledge tools such as Notion and Obsidian, but they care most about ownership, speed, offline work, and trust.

## Product Purpose

Cortex is a local-first, open-source Markdown workspace for writing, organizing, syncing, and extending knowledge that stays in plain files. The landing page should make the product feel real, calm, and technically credible while clearly separating configured release paths from roadmap work. Success is a visitor understanding the ownership model, trusting the sync and plugin stories, and seeing honest availability without unsupported release claims.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and durable. Cortex should feel like a serious workspace with enough warmth to support long writing sessions, but not like a generic SaaS launch page or an over-styled manifesto.

## Anti-references

Avoid AI-slop landing tropes: oversized hero claims, repeated eyebrow labels, endless same-size icon cards, decorative glass panels, noisy gradients, fake metrics, and generic "developer tool" monospace styling. Avoid copying Notion or Obsidian directly; use them as quality references for restraint, clarity, and product confidence.

## Design Principles

- Show the product model before selling the vibe.
- Treat local files, Markdown, sync, and plugins as separate trust stories.
- Make open source feel like product accountability, not repository promotion.
- Keep the page compact, readable, and quietly technical.
- Prefer real interface evidence over decorative explanation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, visible keyboard focus, semantic landmarks, descriptive image alternatives, no motion-gated content, and reduced-motion support for any animation. Download actions must remain clear and stay marked as unavailable until real artifact URLs are configured.
